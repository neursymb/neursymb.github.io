Core Research Areas

Neuro-Symbolic AI research encompasses four primary dimensions, each addressing distinct challenges in creating intelligent systems:

Learning & Inference

Developing hybrid architectures that integrate neural learning with symbolic reasoning. This includes combining neural networks with logic programming, enabling systems to learn from limited data while maintaining logical consistency.

Logic & Reasoning

Integrating formal logic with neural systems using Answer Set Programming, Boolean networks, and constraint satisfaction. Ensures AI decisions are mathematically sound and formally verifiable.

Knowledge Representation

Constructing sophisticated knowledge graphs, ontologies, and semantic networks. Integrating structured knowledge with neural learning to enable systems to understand complex domains and relationships.

Explainability & Trustworthiness

Creating interpretable AI systems whose reasoning processes can be understood and verified. Leveraging symbolic components to provide transparency and build trust with human experts.

Research Focus

Our laboratory focuses on advancing these core areas through both theoretical research and practical applications. We investigate how neural and symbolic components can be effectively integrated, what new capabilities emerge from such integration, and how to ensure these systems remain interpretable and trustworthy.

Integration Challenges

While each area presents unique challenges, the true complexity lies in their integration. Our research addresses questions such as:

  • How can we effectively combine neural learning with symbolic constraints?
  • What architectures best enable knowledge representation and neural processing to work together?
  • How do we maintain explainability while leveraging the power of deep learning?
  • What formal methods can verify the correctness of hybrid neural-symbolic systems?

Future Directions

Our research roadmap includes developing new methodologies that address current limitations, scaling neuro-symbolic approaches to handle real-world complexity, and creating frameworks that practitioners can readily adopt in critical applications.
