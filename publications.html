Walk-In Multi-Stage Patient Flow Scheduling: An ASP Model with DES-Based Evaluation

Authors Pham Ngoc-Mai, Nguyen Trang-Linh, Trinh Van-Giang, Nguyen Ha-Thanh, Vuong Thi-Hai-Yen
Year 2026
Status Accepted

Abstract

This paper addresses the challenge of optimizing patient flow in multi-stage healthcare clinics that accommodate both scheduled appointments and walk-in patients. We present a comprehensive approach combining Answer Set Programming (ASP) for declarative problem modeling with Discrete Event Simulation (DES) for empirical evaluation. Our model captures the complex constraints of real-world clinic operations while maintaining computational tractability.

Key Contributions

  • Hybrid Optimization Framework: Demonstrates effective integration of symbolic reasoning (ASP) with simulation-based evaluation (DES) for healthcare scheduling problems.
  • Walk-In Patient Handling: Develops novel scheduling strategies that accommodate both pre-scheduled appointments and unpredictable walk-in arrivals, a critical real-world scenario often overlooked in academic literature.
  • Multi-Stage Process Modeling: Captures the complexity of multi-stage clinic workflows where patients progress through different service stages with varying resource requirements.
  • Practical Validation: Uses discrete event simulation to evaluate proposed schedules against realistic patient arrival patterns and service time distributions.

Research Insights

Neuro-Symbolic Relevance: This work exemplifies how symbolic reasoning (ASP) can be combined with data-driven simulation to solve complex real-world problems. The approach demonstrates that declarative problem specification through logic programming enables transparent, verifiable solutions while simulation provides empirical validation.

Healthcare Application: Patient scheduling is a critical operational challenge in healthcare systems. By providing interpretable, constraint-based solutions, this approach enables healthcare administrators to understand and trust the scheduling recommendations.

Scalability & Efficiency: The paper addresses the practical challenge of scaling symbolic reasoning approaches to handle realistic problem sizes through effective encoding strategies and simulation-based evaluation.

Artifact & Resources

The complete source code and datasets for this paper are available on Zenodo (DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.19398277), enabling reproducibility and allowing other researchers to build upon this work.
